Chip Drop Challenge

Objective:

Create a package to contain and successfully land your group of astronauts (chips), unbroken from a fall to the ground. You will learn how velocity and acceleration from falling objects relate to a force on landing.

Target Concept:

Acceleration: A vehicle's capacity to gain speed within a short time.
Preparation Time: 5 minutes
Activity Time: 30 minutes
Group size: Teams of three

Materials:

Chips
Parachute material (plastic trash or shopping bags)
Packing material (gelatin, popcorn, foam, bubble wrap, etc.)
Masking tape
Yardstick or meter stick
ANYTHING IN THE WAREHOUSE

Procedure:

Each team of three will build its own lander capsule. You may wish to build more than one for experimentation. Select someone to be a timekeeper, distance measurer and data recorder.
Choose the parachute and packaging material you will use around the chips. Design and build your lander. Attach the parachute.
The landing site will be a 5x5 ft target with varying landing values.
From 12 ft over the target, drop your lander.
Record the distance and time it takes for the chip lander to reach the ground.
Examine and record the lander. A drop is successful if the chips do not crack. You will receive ten points for each surviving chip.
